CASE PRESENATATION

Participants are requested to take original interesting cases worked up by the department of their college.

1.Eligibility: 1st year MBBS to CRRI.

2.Maximum number of participant 1

3.case must be submitted in the form of an abstract with a maximum word limit of 800 words as a ".doc" document.

4.The abstract must contain:

  • Title of abstract (In bold "Times New Roman" with a font size 20)
  • The subheadings should be in bold in "Times New Roman" with a font size 16 and the main text should be in "Times New Roman" with a font size 12
  • Patient details (personal information is not required)
  • Short case history followed by examination findings
  • Investigations (patients privacy is maintained)
  • Differential diagnosis
  • Treatment course
  • References
  • Why this case is relevant/important/ different than usual cases?

5. A full profile picture of the patient is attached to the document with eyes obscured and patient comfortably clothed. In cases pertaining to eyes, the focus should be solely on the eye without including the entire face. Additional evidence of the participant performing examination on the patient is preferred in the form of video (.mp4) or image (.jpg)but not mandatory.

6. Permission letter from the head of the department stating that the case is from his/her department and being presented with his/her knowledge is mandatory. Student details and guide details must be included in the for mentioned letter.

7.The abstract document must be labelled with Name of participant_ college_ subject.

8. Plagiarism of more than 15% is not allowed. If found that the participant has not taken an original case/real case, they will be disqualified.

RESEARCH PRESENATION

1.Eligibility: 1st year MBBS to CRRI. The participant must be one of the co-authors of the paper, Maximum number of participant 1

2.Topic: There is no restriction for the topic of the research article. However the research article must be an Original research conducted by the participants who must be an author with substantial contribution to the study.

3.A scientific committee will review the submitted abstracts; There will be a selection of top abstracts for oral presentation.

4.The remaining abstracts will be considered for poster presentation.

5.The participant must maintain the subjects' confidentiality throughout the presentation.

6.The research paper must not have been published previously in any journal or presented in another conference.

7.Plagiarism must be less than 15%.If found that the participant has not taken an original study, they will be disqualified.

ABSTRACT RULES

Title of abstract (In bold "Times New Roman" with a font size 20) The subheadings should be in bold in "Times New Roman" with a font size 16 and the main text should be in "Times New Roman" with a font size 12 Submitted abstract should contain:

- Word limit: 500

  • Topic without any identifying details such as name of institution
  • Background / Introduction
  • Justification for the topic chosen
  • Objectives
  • Materials and Methods
  • Results
  • Conclusion
  • Implications and limitations
  • References
